Campus Context

Operational context that may affect how scores are interpreted. A scheduled major outdoor event increases the weight of crowd-exposure hazards in the Outdoor Exposure score.

Selecting Yes adjusts Outdoor Exposure score weights based on crowd exposure risk.
Only applies when a major outdoor event is scheduled.
Severe Weather Threat

Check active alerts for the selected county at the NWS office serving that area. Payne County falls under the NWS Norman office. Tulsa and Cherokee counties fall under the NWS Tulsa office. Severe weather outlooks and tornado threat assessments are available from the Storm Prediction Center.

Active lightning within 8 miles forces Outdoor Exposure to Red regardless of other scores.
Flood Hazards

Flash flood watches and warnings are issued by the NWS Norman and NWS Tulsa offices. The Oklahoma Mesonet provides real-time rainfall accumulation data. Flooding directly affects both the Campus Operations and Road & Travel scores.

Winter Hazards

Ice accumulation and wind chill forecasts are available from the NWS Norman or NWS Tulsa area forecast. The Oklahoma Mesonet provides real-time observed temperatures, wind speeds, and precipitation data from stations across the state. Road condition observations from Facilities Management or University Police can supplement forecast data.

Particularly dangerous in morning hours on untreated surfaces.
Heat Hazards

Heat index forecasts are available from the NWS Norman or NWS Tulsa area forecast pages. Both offices publish heat-related decision support information during summer months. Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT) is a more accurate heat stress measure for outdoor activity and is available through Perry Weather for Payne County. If WBGT is entered, it overrides Heat Index in the calculation.

Available from Perry Weather (Payne County only). Preferred over Heat Index when available.
Wind Conditions

Sustained wind and gust forecasts are available from the NWS Norman or NWS Tulsa area forecast and Oklahoma Mesonet current observations. High Wind Warnings and Wind Advisories appear in the NWS active alert feed.

Road & Travel Conditions

Current road conditions for Oklahoma are available at 511.oklahoma.gov. ODOT travel advisories and warnings are issued through the Oklahoma Department of Transportation. Campus walkway and parking lot conditions should be confirmed with Facilities Management or University Police when available.

Based on Facilities Management or University Police ground assessment.
